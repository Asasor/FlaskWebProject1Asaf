The Google Sheets DataSet:
My FRC team: MA5951, recorded the results of all of the matches in our competition and put them
in a google sheet, the results of which can be seen in the Data tab.

The Blue Alliance DataSet:
The Blue Alliance is a database which holds the scoring information for FRC competitions from 1992 to to the present.
Every year the ways to score points change and thus the score quantifiers in the Blue Alliance change from year to year.
This website analyses the 2nd israel competition of 2019.

The score quantifiers for this competition are the following:

  • balls shot into holes (seperated into high, medium and low holes) -- Cargo
  • discs placed on holes (seperated into high, medium and low holes) -- Hatch Panel
  • the level from which the robot descended at the start of a match -- Level
  • the level to which the robot climbed at the end of a match -- Climbing
  • a wheel that the robots must spin a certain distance and then so that
    it is in the right color orientation in order to gain points -- Wheel
Every match in a competition has 2 main stages; teleop and autonomous.
in the autonomous start of the match the robot is not controlled by a human but by autonomous code.
In contrast, during the entirety of the teleop stage the robot is controlled by a human.
during the autonomous stage the robot scores more points for the same actions in the teleop stage

Ranking Score
The Ranking Score is a measure of the ability of the robot; the higher the Ranking Score
the better the robot. This information is very useful as it allows you to compare the performance
of different robots and see what other score quantifiers are related to a high ranking score.
